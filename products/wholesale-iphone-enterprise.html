How Solaris supplies iPhone to enterprise buyers

Solaris allocates iPhone units for institutional buyers through authorised distribution channels, registers as the authorised purchaser in the buyer's Apple Business Manager tenant, kits each unit to the buyer's specification (asset tag, accessory pack, MDM enrolment), and ships either to a depot or direct to the employee with chain-of-custody tracking. Apple's DEP auto-enrolment means every iPhone ordered on the buyer's behalf appears in their ABM for hands-off MDM enrolment on first activation. The employee unboxes, signs in with corporate credentials, and the iPhone is fully managed within 60 seconds.

Apple Business Manager auto-enrolment workflow

Apple Business Manager (DEP) requires the device reseller to be registered as the authorised purchaser in the buyer's enterprise tenant before iPhones are ordered. Once registered, every iPhone Solaris purchases on the buyer's behalf appears in the buyer's ABM for DEP auto-enrolment. The iPhone, on first activation, checks in with Apple's enrolment server, identifies its owner organisation, and pulls the MDM enrolment payload before reaching the home screen. Without this pre-registration, every iPhone requires manual MDM enrolment by IT after receipt; on a 1,000-iPhone deployment that translates to hundreds of hours of avoidable IT staging time.

Solaris's auto-enrolment workflow federates with Jamf Pro, Microsoft Intune, VMware Workspace ONE, Mosyle, Kandji, JumpCloud, MobileIron/Ivanti, IBM MaaS360, Hexnode and most Apple-supporting EMM platforms. Detailed coverage in Zero-Touch and Apple Business Manager reseller.
