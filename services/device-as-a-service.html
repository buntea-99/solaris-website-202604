What Device-as-a-Service actually means

Device-as-a-Service is a procurement model where the buyer pays a per-device monthly fee that covers the device itself, custom OS imaging, MDM enrolment, kitting, direct-to-employee fulfillment, in-life support and replacement, and end-of-life reverse logistics. Capital cost moves to operating expense. The supplier carries inventory, lifecycle replacement, refurbishment and disposal. The buyer's IT team manages the policy and the workforce, not the supply chain.

Solaris's DaaS model is built for institutional buyers who already understand the cost of running device procurement, MDM, fulfilment, replacement and disposal as four separate contracts with four separate suppliers. Consolidating those into a single per-device monthly fee typically reduces total cost of ownership by 12-22% on a 500-10,000 device fleet.

What is included in Solaris DaaS

  • Device hardware: the agreed device model, refreshed at end-of-term to the equivalent then-current model.
  • Custom OS imaging: Android open-source flashing, vendor-locked OS, carrier-specific firmware, splash-screen branding.
  • MDM auto-enrolment: registration in your Apple Business Manager, Google Zero-Touch or Samsung Knox tenant.
  • Kitting and fulfillment: per-employee kit packed with accessories, asset-tagged, shipped direct to the employee with chain-of-custody tracking.
  • In-life replacement: warranty management, accidental damage replacement to the agreed SLA (next-business-day, second-business-day, in-week), pre-imaged replacement shipped direct to employee.
  • Help-desk pass-through: Tier-1 device-handling escalation routed to Solaris for hardware-class issues so the buyer's IT team focuses on the policy stack.
  • Reverse logistics: off-boarding retrieval, secure data wipe to NIST 800-88, refurbish-and-redeploy or certified e-waste disposal.
  • End-of-term refresh: coordinated swap of the entire fleet to the next-generation model at the end of the term.

How Solaris DaaS pricing works

Pricing is a per-device per-month fee scoped to: device model, term length (24, 36 or 48 months), MDM platform, fulfilment model (direct-to-employee or depot), in-life replacement SLA tier, accessory bundle, and end-of-life option (refurbish-and-redeploy or certified disposal). Solaris quotes per-device pricing within one business day of receiving the scope. There is no upfront capital outlay; the fee starts the month the first device ships.

For MVNO and carrier engagements, the per-device fee can be bundled with SIM activation and carrier connectivity into a single per-subscriber per-month fee covering hardware, connectivity and lifecycle.

DaaS versus outright purchase: which fits your organisation

DaaS fits organisations that want predictable per-device operating cost, a single supplier carrying procurement plus lifecycle plus disposal, and faster end-of-term refresh cycles. Outright purchase fits organisations with capital budget headroom, in-house IT staging capacity, a long-tail device mix the buyer prefers to manage internally, or compliance requirements that mandate ownership of the asset.

Solaris offers both engagement models. For mid-size enterprise fleets (500-10,000 devices) with hybrid or distributed workforces, DaaS typically reduces total cost of ownership by 12-22% versus DIY procurement plus separate MDM, fulfilment and disposal contracts. For specialist hardware (Sonim, Kyocera, Zebra, body cameras) or sub-500 device fleets, outright purchase often wins on TCO; Solaris quotes both side-by-side at proposal stage.
