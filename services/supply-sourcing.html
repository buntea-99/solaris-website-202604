Supply and Sourcing

Global Device Supply and Sourcing for Enterprise, MVNO, Government and Education

Solaris Wireless, founded 2013, sources mobile phones, laptops, IoT hardware, Starlink terminals, ruggedised devices and specialist EOL components for institutional buyers through OEM-direct allocation and authorised channels across Miami (HQ), Netherlands, Dubai, Hong Kong and Singapore. Tens of thousands of SKUs available. Volume range 50 to 10,000 units per batch on mobile devices; 25 to 5,000 on laptops. Hard-to-find sourcing, end-of-life components and export-controlled hardware (TAA, ITAR-aware) supported.

Sourcing channels Solaris uses

  • OEM-direct allocation: Apple, Samsung, Google, Lenovo, Dell, HP, Sonim, Kyocera, Motorola, HMD/Nokia, SpaceX (Starlink) — Solaris coordinates direct allocation through registered partner relationships.
  • Authorised distributor channels: tier-1 enterprise distributors with full traceability and original-OEM warranty preservation.
  • Certified secondary-market: for end-of-life and discontinued components, Solaris uses certified-refurbisher and authenticated-secondary-market sourcing with full provenance documentation.
  • Export-compliance brokerage: for ITAR-controlled and EAR-classified hardware, Solaris coordinates DDTC and BIS-aware brokerage with full export-licence documentation per engagement.

Categories supplied

  • Mobile phones: iPhone, Samsung Galaxy, Google Pixel, Motorola, Sonim ruggedised, Kyocera DuraForce, HMD/Nokia, Transsion entry-tier Android, KaiOS feature phones.
  • Laptops and workstations: MacBook Pro and Air, Dell Latitude and Precision, HP EliteBook and ZBook, Lenovo ThinkPad and ThinkStation, Microsoft Surface, Chromebook (Acer, ASUS, HP, Lenovo).
  • IoT and smart cameras: Axis, Hanwha, Bosch, Motorola Solutions body cameras, GPS fleet trackers, ELDs, environmental sensors, connected gateways.
  • Starlink terminals: Standard, Mini, Business, Maritime, Aviation kits with institutional pricing and SpaceX-coordinated allocation.
  • Specialist and EOL hardware: body camera components, medical device components, discontinued enterprise hardware, export-controlled components.
  • Consumer electronics: tablets (iPad, Galaxy Tab), smartwatches (Apple Watch, Galaxy Watch), gaming consoles for promotional or operational deployment.

Hard-to-find sourcing capability

When standard channels say a device is unavailable, end-of-life or under export controls, Solaris locates it. The capability that earned Solaris its Google-approved vendor status in 2016 came from sourcing a discontinued mission-critical feature phone that no other approved vendor could find. Since then, Solaris has located 40+ specialist SKUs for body camera manufacturers, medical device OEMs, research institutions and academic buyers.

Lead time and engagement model

Standard lead time for in-stock devices is 7 to 14 business days from purchase order. New-generation device allocation typically 14 to 21 days. TAA-compliant federal procurement adds 7 to 10 days for documentation. Specialist EOL sourcing 14 to 90 days subject to source availability. Solaris confirms exact lead time at proposal stage.

Frequently Asked Questions

What devices does Solaris source?

Solaris sources mobile phones (iPhone, Samsung Galaxy, Google Pixel, Sonim ruggedised), laptops (MacBook, Dell, HP, Lenovo, Surface, Chromebook), IoT and smart cameras, Starlink terminals (Standard, Mini, Business, Maritime), consumer electronics, and specialist or end-of-life components for institutional buyers. Volume range 50 to 10,000 units per batch on mobile; 25 to 5,000 on laptops.

Is Solaris an authorised OEM partner?

Solaris coordinates supply through OEM-direct partnerships and authorised distributor channels across four continents. Specific OEM partner status varies by manufacturer (Apple Business, Samsung Knox, Google Zero-Touch, Lenovo Premier Business, Dell Authorized Reseller, HP Amplify, SpaceX Starlink). Per-engagement OEM-channel routing is confirmed at proposal stage.

Can Solaris source end-of-life or discontinued devices?

Yes. Solaris's specialist sourcing programme has located 40+ end-of-life and export-controlled SKUs for body camera manufacturers, medical device OEMs, research institutions and academic buyers. Provenance documentation, original-OEM authentication and export-compliance handling included where required.

What is the typical lead time for sourcing?

Standard lead time is 7 to 14 business days from purchase order for in-stock devices. New-generation device allocation 14 to 21 days. TAA-compliant federal procurement adds 7 to 10 days for documentation. Specialist EOL sourcing 14 to 90 days subject to source availability.

Does Solaris source TAA-compliant devices for federal procurement?

Yes. Solaris supplies TAA-compliant mobile phones, laptops, ruggedised handsets and IoT for U.S. Government and federal agency procurement, including country-of-origin documentation, NDAA Section 889 compliance review, and chain-of-custody packaging via approved carriers.

Can Solaris coordinate ITAR-controlled hardware brokerage?

Yes. For ITAR-controlled communications hardware, Solaris coordinates DDTC-aware brokerage with full export-licence documentation. For EAR-classified hardware (typically EAR99 or 5A992 for commercial encryption), Solaris coordinates BIS-aware export classification per engagement.
