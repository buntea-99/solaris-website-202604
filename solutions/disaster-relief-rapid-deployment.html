72-hour deployment is a different supply problem

When a hurricane lands, an earthquake hits or a humanitarian crisis triggers a response operation, the NGO or response agency cannot wait 4 weeks for a procurement cycle. Devices need to be on a plane within 72 hours, pre-configured, with the right connectivity, asset-tagged and ready for field staff to power on.

Solaris Wireless maintains pre-cleared inventory for rapid deployment. The Miami headquarters and the Netherlands and Singapore supply nodes hold ready-to-ship inventory of smartphones, Starlink kits, satellite communicators and ruggedised laptops for exactly this scenario.

Solaris capabilities for disaster relief and NGO operations

Solaris has supported U.S. Government, allied nations and humanitarian programmes with 72-hour rapid response for ruggedised communications hardware. The standard rapid-deploy package includes smartphones (pre-configured with offline-capable mapping, secure messaging, and the agency's MDM enrolment), Starlink kits (pre-registered to the agency's service account), satellite communicators (Iridium, Inmarsat) for areas without Starlink coverage, and ruggedised laptops for field offices.

International logistics is handled end-to-end including export-control compliance (ITAR, EAR), customs documentation for the receiving country, and last-mile delivery to the staging location, base camp or field office.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions from buyers

How quickly can Solaris ship pre-configured devices for emergency response?

Solaris Wireless maintains pre-cleared inventory at multiple supply nodes for 72-hour rapid response. For standard rapid-deploy packages, devices ship within 72 hours of order confirmation.

Does Solaris handle export-control compliance for international NGO deployments?

Yes. Solaris handles ITAR, EAR and destination-country export compliance for controlled hardware deployment to international operations including humanitarian response in restricted regions.

Can devices be pre-configured with offline-capable applications?

Yes. Devices ship with offline-capable mapping (Maps.me, OsmAnd, agency-specific GIS apps), secure messaging (Signal, Wickr), and offline forms applications pre-installed and configured.

What satellite communication options does Solaris supply?

Solaris supplies Starlink Standard, Business and Maritime kits, Iridium GO! and Iridium Extreme satellite phones, Inmarsat IsatPhone and BGAN terminals, and Garmin inReach satellite messengers.
