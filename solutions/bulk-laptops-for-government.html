Government laptop procurement requires specialist handling

Federal civilian and defence agencies cannot buy laptops from generic distribution. The Trade Agreements Act requires country-of-origin documentation. FIPS encryption requires validated cryptographic modules. CAC/PIV authentication requires the right smart card reader, configured to the agency's PKI. ITAR-controlled deployments require chain-of-custody documentation from manufacturer to end user.

State and local government layer in additional requirements: GSA Schedule pricing, NASPO ValuePoint contract alignment, state cybersecurity policy compliance. Solaris Wireless coordinates these requirements as a baseline part of every government engagement.

Solaris Wireless capabilities for government

Solaris supplies TAA-compliant laptops from Dell, Lenovo, HP and Apple through authorised commercial channels with country-of-origin documentation. FIPS-validated encryption is enabled at provisioning with TPM-bound keys. CAC/PIV reader configuration is loaded against the agency's PKI before shipment.

For deployments under ITAR, EAR or other export control regimes, Solaris handles the controlled-shipment workflow including DSP-83 documentation where required, end-user certification, and chain-of-custody tracking. The U.S. Government and allied nations have used Solaris for ruggedised communications hardware deployment with 72-hour rapid response.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions from buyers

Is Solaris Wireless a GSA-listed vendor?

Solaris Wireless coordinates supply through GSA Schedule holders and direct-to-agency contracts. For agency procurement teams that require specific contract vehicle alignment, our team works with your contracting officer to identify the correct path.

Are the laptops TAA-compliant?

Yes. All government-channel laptops are sourced from TAA-designated countries with country-of-origin documentation included in the order. The TAA assertion is part of the standard government delivery package.

Can Solaris configure CAC/PIV readers and smart cards?

Yes. We pre-configure CAC/PIV smart card readers, integrate with your agency PKI, and set the smart card middleware (ActivClient, Identity Device Manager) before shipment so the laptop authenticates on first connection to the agency network.

Does Solaris handle ITAR and EAR controlled deployments?

Yes. Solaris handles ITAR/EAR controlled hardware deployments with full chain-of-custody documentation, DSP-83 where required, and end-user certification. We have served U.S. Government and allied nations programmes for ruggedised communications hardware.
