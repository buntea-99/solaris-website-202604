Smart-city procurement requires deployment-grade supply

A municipal smart-city deployment is not a 50-camera order. It is 800 cameras, 2,400 environmental sensors and 60 gateways across 12 neighbourhoods, each with a specific certification requirement (FCC, CE, IC), each with municipal asset tagging, each pre-paired to a backhaul plan.

Solaris Wireless supplies at this resolution. Bulk orders break out by neighbourhood and asset class; each unit is FCC-documented, asset-tagged to the municipal scheme, and pre-paired to the right backhaul (cellular SIM, LoRaWAN gateway, or fibre uplink). Field-installation kits travel with the device.

Solaris capabilities for smart-city programmes

Solaris sources IoT cameras and sensors from established OEMs (Axis, Hikvision, Bosch, Hanwha for cameras; Libelium, Vaisala, Bosch for sensors) through authorised channels with full FCC equipment authorisation documentation. For specialist sensors, the supply chain extends to European and Asian manufacturers via our Netherlands and Hong Kong supply nodes.

Configuration includes firmware versioning to your IT-OT baseline, MAC address registration with your municipal network, NTP and SNMP configuration, and integration credentials for your IoT platform (AWS IoT Core, Azure IoT Hub, ThingsBoard, ThingWorx).

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions from buyers

Does Solaris supply Hikvision and Dahua despite NDAA restrictions?

Solaris supplies cameras to fit the procurement requirements of the deploying agency. For NDAA-restricted programmes we route to NDAA-compliant alternatives (Axis, Bosch, Hanwha, Verkada). For programmes outside NDAA scope, the full vendor catalogue is available.

Can sensors be pre-paired to our IoT platform?

Yes. We pre-pair devices to AWS IoT Core, Azure IoT Hub, Google Cloud IoT, ThingsBoard, ThingWorx and most major municipal IoT platforms with the correct credentials and certificates loaded before shipment.

What backhaul options does Solaris support?

Cellular (LTE-M, NB-IoT, 5G FWA), LoRaWAN, fibre, and Starlink for remote locations. Solaris also supplies the Starlink kits for hard-to-reach municipal infrastructure where cellular coverage is poor.

Does Solaris supply field-installation hardware with the cameras?

Yes. Pole-mount brackets, junction boxes, weatherproof enclosures, surge protectors and PoE injectors ship as part of the deployment kit so the field crew has everything needed in one delivery.
