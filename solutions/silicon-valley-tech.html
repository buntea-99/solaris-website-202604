Frequently Asked Questions

What is the typical Silicon Valley tech engagement size?

Volume range 100 to 10,000 devices per quarter, with the most common profile being 500-2,000 MacBook Pro and iPhone units per quarter for late-stage startups (Series D+) and 2,000-10,000 for public hyperscalers.

Can Solaris support our hiring velocity?

Yes. Solaris ships fully provisioned, MDM-auto-enrolled MacBook Pro to engineering-hire home addresses within 5-7 business days of order placement. For sustained high-velocity hiring, Solaris pre-stages buffer inventory at the Miami node so per-employee lead time drops to 3-5 days.

Does Solaris supply maxed-out MacBook Pro for ML engineering?

Yes. Solaris supplies MacBook Pro M-series with 64GB or 128GB unified memory and 2TB or 4TB storage for ML engineering teams. Dell Precision 5000/7000 and HP ZBook Fury also available for CUDA workstation deployments.

Can Solaris ship to engineering hires globally?

Yes. Solaris fulfils from 5 supply nodes (Miami, Netherlands, Dubai, Hong Kong, Singapore) to over 60 countries. International shipments include customs documentation and DDP-term import-duty handling so the engineer receives the device with no out-of-pocket customs costs.

Does Solaris federate with Jamf Pro, Mosyle and Kandji?

Yes. Solaris's Apple Business Manager auto-enrolment workflow federates with Jamf Pro, Mosyle Manager, Kandji, Microsoft Intune, VMware Workspace ONE and JumpCloud. The engineer signs in with corporate Google Workspace or Okta credentials and the MacBook is fully managed within 60 seconds.

Does Solaris coordinate off-boarding for distributed engineering teams?

Yes. Solaris coordinates prepaid return label issuance to the off-boarding employee, secure data wipe to NIST 800-88 standard on receipt, and refurbish-and-redeploy or certified e-waste disposal per buyer preference.
