01 — Supply

Global hardware sourcing at institutional scale

Solaris Wireless maintains active sourcing relationships with OEMs, authorised distributors and strategic partners across North America, Europe, the Middle East and Asia Pacific. Our supply network gives institutional buyers access to tens of thousands of SKUs — from flagship smartphones and ruggedised laptops to Starlink satellite terminals, IoT sensors, gaming consoles and next-generation AI wearables.

How It Works

Every engagement begins with a detailed requirements analysis. We identify the exact device specifications, quantities, regional compliance needs and delivery timeline. Our sourcing team then activates the optimal supply channel — whether that means pulling from existing inventory, placing a direct OEM order, or leveraging our grey-market network for hard-to-find or end-of-life hardware.

What We Source

  • Mobile phones & smartphones: Apple iPhone, Samsung Galaxy, Google Pixel, Motorola, Nokia and budget Android devices for emerging-market MVNOs. We supply carrier-locked, unlocked and refurbished units at volumes from 100 to 100,000+.
  • Laptops & Chromebooks: Enterprise-grade HP, Dell, Lenovo and Apple MacBook devices for corporate fleets, education deployments and government programmes. Chromebook sourcing for school districts at scale.
  • IoT devices & sensors: Connected hardware for smart city infrastructure, fleet management, agriculture monitoring and industrial automation. We source from Qualcomm, Sierra Wireless, Digi International and specialist OEMs.
  • Starlink terminals: SpaceX Starlink satellite internet kits for remote government installations, maritime deployments and rural connectivity programmes.
  • Gaming consoles & accessories: PlayStation, Xbox, Nintendo Switch and Meta Quest units for institutional buyers, hospitality chains, military recreation programmes and retail distribution.
  • AI glasses & wearables: Next-generation smart glasses and AI-enabled wearables from Meta, Google, and emerging manufacturers for enterprise field operations and pilot programmes.

Use Cases

A Pacific island MVNO needed 15,000 Android handsets with specific regional band support and carrier branding — sourced and delivered within 6 weeks. A Fortune 500 company required 2,000 ruggedised tablets for field technicians across 12 countries, each with country-specific power adapters and documentation. A government education ministry procured 50,000 Chromebooks for a national schools programme, requiring phased delivery across 18 months.

02 — Provisioning

Every device configured to your exact specification

Provisioning is where raw hardware becomes a deployable asset. Solaris Wireless operates dedicated provisioning facilities where every unit is individually configured to meet the client's precise technical requirements — from operating system builds and carrier-specific firmware to SIM lock policies, network parameters and pre-installed application suites. No device leaves our facility until it has been tested, validated and confirmed ready for immediate use.

Provisioning Capabilities

OS FlashingCustom SoftwareSIM LockCarrier BannersMMS ParametersApp Pre-install
  • OS flashing & firmware deployment: Custom Android ROM builds, iOS MDM enrolment profiles, Windows Autopilot configuration and ChromeOS enterprise enrolment. We flash devices individually or at scale using automated provisioning stations.
  • SIM lock configuration: Carrier-specific SIM lock and unlock policies, including multi-IMSI provisioning for MVNOs that require devices locked to their network at first boot.
  • Carrier banners & branding: Custom boot animations, carrier logos, home screen layouts and operator-branded system applications that make each device distinctly yours.
  • MMS & APN parameters: Pre-configured messaging and data settings so devices connect to the correct carrier network without requiring end-user setup or manual APN entry.
  • App pre-installation: Enterprise applications, MDM agents, productivity suites and custom software loaded and configured before the device ships. We support both system-level and user-level application installation.
  • Custom software & configuration: Kiosk mode lockdowns, single-app configurations, Wi-Fi and VPN profile pre-configuration, and security policy enforcement for managed device deployments.

The Process

Clients provide a provisioning specification document — or we help create one. Our engineering team builds a provisioning profile that is tested against a sample batch before full-scale deployment begins. Each device passes through a multi-point quality assurance check: power-on test, network registration verification, application launch confirmation and physical inspection. Devices that fail any checkpoint are flagged, re-provisioned or replaced.

Use Cases

Republic Wireless required 25,000 Android devices with custom ROM builds, locked SIM policies and pre-installed carrier applications — all configured and shipped within tight seasonal deadlines. A restaurant technology platform needed 10,000 tablets provisioned with a proprietary kiosk application, device management software and custom boot sequences for deployment across three continents. Google engaged Solaris to provision internal testing devices with pre-release firmware builds under strict security and chain-of-custody protocols.

03 — Fulfilment

From warehouse to end user, managed end-to-end

Solaris Wireless manages the complete logistics chain from our facilities to the final destination — whether that is a corporate headquarters in New York, a retail store in Sydney, a government office in Dubai, or a remote village school on a Pacific island. Our fulfilment operations are built for the complexity that institutional deployments demand: multi-country customs compliance, phased delivery schedules, real-time tracking and white-glove handling for sensitive hardware.

Fulfilment Services

Direct-to-CustomerFreight ForwardingConsignmentRetail PackagingKitting
  • Direct-to-customer delivery: Individual device shipments to end users, employees or retail locations. We support same-day dispatch, next-day delivery within the continental US, and international express shipping to 120+ countries.
  • Freight forwarding: Palletised bulk shipments via air freight, ocean freight or ground transport. We manage customs documentation, duties, import compliance and last-mile coordination for large-scale international deployments.
  • Consignment holding: Inventory staged at our facilities or partner warehouses, released in tranches on your schedule. Ideal for phased rollouts, seasonal promotions or buffer stock programmes where you need devices ready to ship on 24-hour notice.
  • Retail packaging: Branded retail boxes, accessory bundling, multilingual documentation inserts and shelf-ready packaging for devices entering retail distribution channels.
  • Kitting: Combining multiple components — device, charger, cable, SIM card, documentation, protective case — into a single shipment-ready package. Every kit is assembled, quality-checked and sealed to your specification.

Logistics Capabilities

Our fulfilment infrastructure supports both high-volume bulk operations and granular direct-to-user distribution. We maintain relationships with major carriers including FedEx, DHL, UPS and regional last-mile providers. Every shipment receives a tracking number, and clients have access to a real-time dashboard showing order status, delivery confirmation and exception alerts. For government and defence clients, we provide chain-of-custody documentation and secure transport protocols.

Use Cases

Ritual.co required 10,000 kiosk units delivered to restaurant locations across North America, Europe and Australia — each with site-specific configurations and accessories. Delivery was coordinated with on-site installation teams to minimise restaurant downtime. A military client needed ruggedised devices shipped to forward operating bases with full customs pre-clearance and tamper-evident packaging. An MVNO launched a new handset across 200 retail locations simultaneously, requiring coordinated delivery timed to a marketing campaign launch date.

04 — Kitting & Assembly

Deploy-ready units, assembled to your exact standard

Kitting and assembly is the final transformation that turns provisioned hardware into a deployment-ready product. Solaris Wireless handles complete device preparation — mounting devices on stands or fixtures, applying retail packaging and branded labels, bundling accessories and documentation, and performing final quality assurance checks. When a unit leaves our facility, it is ready to be placed directly into the hands of the end user or onto a retail shelf with zero additional handling required.

Assembly Services

Stand MountingRetail PackagingLabelingDeployable UnitsQuality Assurance
  • Stand & fixture mounting: Tablets and kiosk devices mounted onto countertop stands, wall brackets, floor pedestals or custom enclosures. Each unit is secured, tested for stability and verified for cable routing and power connection.
  • Retail packaging: Devices placed into branded retail boxes with foam inserts, accessory compartments and shrink-wrap sealing. We produce packaging that meets retail presentation standards for major chains and e-commerce fulfilment.
  • Labeling & asset tagging: Serial number labels, asset tags, regulatory compliance stickers, barcodes and QR codes applied to devices and packaging. We support both pre-printed labels and on-demand printing for variable data.
  • Deployable unit creation: Combining the device, stand or mount, power supply, network cables, SIM cards and printed materials into a single deployable unit. Each unit is a self-contained package that can be unboxed and activated at the deployment site without technical expertise.
  • Quality assurance & validation: Every assembled unit undergoes a final inspection checklist: power-on test, software verification, physical inspection, accessory completeness check and packaging integrity confirmation. Units that fail any checkpoint are reworked before release.

The Process

Assembly begins with a detailed work instruction document that specifies every component, its placement and the assembly sequence. Our team builds pilot units for client approval before full production begins. Assembly is performed on dedicated workstations with component staging, ESD protection and photographic documentation of each completed unit. Batch tracking ensures full traceability from individual components to the finished deployable unit.

Use Cases

Ritual.co's kiosk deployment required tablets mounted on branded countertop stands with integrated charging, sealed in custom shipping boxes with installation guides — 10,000 units assembled in our facility over 4 months. A national pharmacy chain needed 3,000 patient check-in tablets mounted on adjustable floor stands with privacy screens, each pre-configured and ready for plug-and-play installation. A government census programme required 8,000 ruggedised tablets bundled with portable power banks, styluses and waterproof cases in labelled field kits for distribution to census workers across remote regions.
