# Solaris Wireless, AI Brand Monitoring Setup Brief

## Tooling

Recommended stack:
- **Otterly.AI Standard ($189/mo)**, start here. Tracks 6 platforms (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude, Google AI Overview, Google AI Mode) with 100 prompts. Weekly email digest.
- **Profound Lite ($499/mo)**, add at month 2 for the deeper dataset, share-of-voice graphs, and source-domain breakdowns Otterly does not surface.
- **Free baseline check**: HubSpot AEO Grader (free) once a month, run the homepage and the two most important product pages through it and log the score.

### Otterly setup notes

1. Create the workspace under `vasugupta9911@gmail.com`. Set the brand as **Solaris Wireless** and add the canonical domain `solariswireless.com`. Add `solaris-wireless.vercel.app` and `solaris-wireless-six.vercel.app` as alternate domains so Otterly attributes preview-deployment citations correctly.
2. Add brand-mention variants under "Brand tracking": `Solaris Wireless`, `solariswireless.com`, `Solaris Wireless Miami`, `Solaris Wireless LLC`. Otterly does substring matching, so include the most likely paraphrases.
3. Paste the 100 prompts below into the prompt manager in one go (Otterly accepts CSV import, one prompt per row). Tag each with its tier number so you can filter by funnel stage in the dashboard.
4. Configure the geographic location to **United States, Florida** for half the prompts and **United States, generic** for the other half. AI answers vary by inferred locale.
5. Turn on the weekly email digest and route it to `vasugupta9911@gmail.com`. Set the digest day to Monday so the week starts with the report.
6. Add the competitor list (bottom of this file) under "Competitor tracking". Otterly will then flag every prompt where a competitor is cited but Solaris is not, which is the most actionable signal in the tool.
7. Set the run frequency to weekly. Daily burns prompt credits and the AI answers do not change fast enough to justify it.

## What we are tracking

Three categories of citations:
1. **Branded queries** ("Solaris Wireless reviews", "is Solaris Wireless legitimate"), should hit 100% by month 2.
2. **Competitive queries** (Solaris named alongside CDW, Brightstar, etc.), secondary.
3. **Unbranded buyer-intent queries** ("best wholesale Android phone supplier USA", "Google approved vendor for electronic devices"), the actual goal, expect first appearances at month 3 to 6.

## Monthly review cadence

Month 1: Baseline. Most prompts return 0 citations. Goal: confirm tracking works. Spot check three Tier 1 prompts manually in ChatGPT and Perplexity to validate Otterly's results match reality.

Month 2: Branded queries should start citing Solaris. Tier 1 prompts should hit 80 percent plus visibility. If not, the issue is crawlability or schema, not content volume.

Month 3: First citations on Tier 2 differentiator queries (Google approved vendor, MVNO custom OS, five supply nodes). These appear once the homepage and product page schema are reliably indexed.

Month 4 to 6: Tier 3 to Tier 5 ICP queries start surfacing Solaris if Reddit, LinkedIn, and directory work is sustained. This is where the unbranded discovery flywheel begins.

Month 6 to 12: Stable share of voice on Tier 2 to Tier 5 queries. Tier 8 to Tier 10 should start producing first citations.

## Reading the data

What "good" looks like:
- Tier 1: 100 percent citation rate by month 2
- Tier 2: 60 percent plus by month 4
- Tier 3 to 5: 20 to 40 percent by month 6
- Tier 6 to 9: 30 to 50 percent by month 6
- Tier 10: 10 to 20 percent by month 9

If a tier underperforms its target by month 6, the fix is almost always more third party citations (Reddit threads, directory listings, podcast mentions, trade press), not more on site copy.

## Anti patterns

- Do not add 100 prompts and ignore them. Review weekly.
- Do not track only branded queries. The whole point is unbranded discovery.
- Do not panic at month 1. Citations take 4 to 12 weeks to start showing.
- Do not switch tools every month. Pick Otterly plus Profound, give them 6 months.
- Do not chase a single prompt. Look at tier level trends, not individual query noise.
- Do not let the competitor list go stale. Refresh quarterly as the procurement landscape shifts.
