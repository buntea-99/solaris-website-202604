How Solaris distributes Apple to institutional buyers

Solaris Wireless allocates Apple devices for institutional buyers through authorised channel relationships and direct OEM allocation. Each engagement runs end-to-end: OEM allocation, custom provisioning, asset tagging, MDM auto-enrolment registration, accessory pairing, tamper-evident packaging, and direct-to-employee or depot fulfilment with chain-of-custody tracking. Volume range 50 to 10,000 units per batch for mobile devices; 25 to 5,000 units for laptops and workstations.

Apple-specific capabilities Solaris layers on top of the standard distribution workflow: Apple Business Manager (ABM) authorised-reseller registration, Apple School Manager (ASM) registration for educational buyers, AppleCare for Enterprise coordination, eSIM provisioning, original Apple warranty preservation, factory-unlocked supply for international and MVNO deployment, Apple Lightning to USB-C accessory bundles per device generation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Solaris Wireless an authorised Apple distributor?

Solaris Wireless allocates Apple devices for institutional buyers through authorised channels and registers as the authorised purchaser in the buyer's Apple Business Manager (or Apple School Manager for educational buyers) tenant. Every Apple device ordered on the buyer's behalf appears in their ABM for DEP auto-enrolment.

What Apple devices does Solaris supply for enterprise procurement?

Solaris supplies iPhone (16, 15, 14, SE), iPad (Pro, Air, mini, standard), MacBook Pro and Air (M-series silicon), Apple Watch (Series and Ultra), iMac, Mac Mini and Apple TV. All units shipped with original Apple warranty and registered in your ABM or ASM tenant.

Does Solaris register Apple devices in my Apple Business Manager tenant?

Yes. Solaris registers as the authorised purchaser in your Apple Business Manager tenant. Every Apple device ordered on your behalf appears in your ABM for DEP auto-enrolment. The employee unboxes, signs in with corporate credentials, and the device is fully managed by your MDM (Jamf Pro, Microsoft Intune, VMware Workspace ONE, Mosyle, Kandji, JumpCloud) within 60 seconds.

Can Solaris coordinate AppleCare for Enterprise?

Yes. Solaris coordinates AppleCare for Enterprise on bulk Apple deployments, including Tier-1 device-handling support tier negotiation, hardware replacement SLA configuration and lifecycle warranty management. The buyer's IT team focuses on policy and the workforce; Solaris coordinates AppleCare engagement.

Can Solaris supply factory-unlocked iPhone for MVNO and international deployment?

Yes. Solaris supplies factory-unlocked iPhone units for MVNO subscriber-acquisition programmes and international deployment. SIM activation, eSIM provisioning and MVNO carrier-profile installation are coordinated alongside the order. Solaris has supplied unlocked iPhone for Republic Wireless and Pacific MVNO operators including Cook Islands operators.

What is the lead time for a bulk Apple order?

Standard lead time is 7 to 14 business days for in-stock Apple models. New-generation iPhone or MacBook (within 90 days of release) is 14 to 21 days subject to Apple allocation. TAA-compliant federal procurement adds 7 to 10 days. Educational ASM deployments follow the same lead times.
