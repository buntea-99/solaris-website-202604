The Challenge

A Hard Deadline. 500 Sites. No Room for Error.

A high-growth retail chain needed to deploy self-service kiosk hardware across 500 locations ahead of a critical commercial launch. The 90-day window was non-negotiable — missing it meant losing a seasonal revenue window worth tens of millions in projected GMV.

The kiosks required custom hardware configurations: specific display sizes, ruggedised enclosures, pre-loaded software, and bespoke peripheral integrations. No off-the-shelf solution existed. Standard procurement channels quoted 20-week lead times. That wasn't an option.

"Every day we slipped past our launch date was revenue we couldn't recover. We needed a partner who understood urgency the same way we did."
Our Solution

Parallel Sourcing, Staged Configuration, Direct Delivery

Solaris Wireless activated a parallel sourcing strategy — engaging four manufacturers simultaneously to compress lead times and build redundancy into the supply chain. We maintained a buffer stock of 15% above requirement at each provisioning stage to absorb any attrition without impacting the delivery schedule.

Configuration was handled in three geographically distributed staging hubs, allowing us to pre-position inventory closer to delivery clusters and reduce final-mile transit time. Each unit was tested against a 47-point QA checklist before dispatch.

01
Hardware Specification & Sourcing

Worked with the client's product team to finalise hardware spec within 72 hours. Simultaneously engaged four qualified manufacturers, securing best-price commitments on aggregate volume.

02
Multi-Hub Staging Infrastructure

Established three staging hubs across the deployment geography. Inventory was pre-positioned to reduce last-mile distance and enable same-week delivery to any cluster within each region.

03
Configuration & QA at Scale

Each kiosk flashed with client firmware, peripherals tested, 47-point checklist completed. Units failing any check were immediately flagged and replaced from buffer stock without touching the schedule.

04
Coordinated Site Delivery

Deliveries coordinated with each store's operations team. Tracking provided per unit, per site. All 500 locations received hardware within a 12-day final delivery window.

The Outcome

On Time. Zero Defects. Client Launched on Schedule.

All 500 kiosks were delivered, configured, and operational before the client's launch date. No defects were reported at deployment. The client opened all locations on the same day — their largest coordinated launch to date. Solaris Wireless was retained for the Phase 2 rollout covering an additional 200 international sites.
