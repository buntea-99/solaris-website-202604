The Challenge

Six Operators. One Supply Chain.

When a consortium of European and North American MVNOs sought a unified supply partner, the requirement was clear: every handset must arrive SIM-locked, carrier-branded, and network-ready — at volume, and on schedule.

The operators ranged from a 200,000-subscriber discount carrier in Germany to a fast-growing IoT-focused MVNO in the United States. Each had distinct firmware requirements, unique UI overlays, and separate regulatory obligations. Managing six independent procurement relationships was costing them time, capital, and operational focus.

"We needed a single throat to choke — one partner who could take our spec sheet and deliver thousands of ready-to-activate units across borders without us babysitting the process."
Our Solution

Centralised Sourcing with Per-Operator Configuration

Solaris Wireless established a dedicated provisioning pipeline for the consortium — consolidating purchasing power while maintaining strict per-operator configuration isolation. We sourced devices from manufacturers across Asia and Europe, negotiating unified pricing that reflected the aggregate volume.

Our warehouse provisioning team built configuration profiles for each operator in advance, enabling parallel flashing workflows. Devices were physically separated by operator lot from arrival, eliminating cross-contamination risk between firmware profiles.

01
Operator Specification Audit

Collected firmware, branding, SIM-lock, and regulatory requirements from all six operators. Resolved conflicts and standardised documentation into a unified provisioning schema.

02
Consolidated Sourcing

Negotiated volume pricing across three manufacturers, reducing per-unit cost by 18% versus individual operator purchasing. Arranged staggered delivery windows aligned to each operator's launch calendar.

03
Parallel Provisioning at Scale

Deployed six isolated provisioning stations — one per operator — enabling simultaneous flashing of different firmware profiles without process interference or delay.

04
Kitting & Direct Fulfilment

Devices kitted with operator-branded accessories and documentation, then shipped direct to operator distribution hubs — bypassing intermediate warehousing and cutting lead time by 11 days.

The Outcome

On-Time, On-Spec, Under Budget

All six operators received their full device allocations within agreed windows. Provisioning error rate across the entire programme was under 0.3% — well below the industry benchmark of 2%. The consortium renewed the supply agreement for a second year and expanded scope to include tablet devices.
