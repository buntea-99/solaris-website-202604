What auto-enrolment requires from a device reseller

Apple Business Manager (DEP), Google Zero-Touch Enrollment, and Samsung Knox Mobile Enrollment all require the device reseller to be registered as the authorised purchaser in your enterprise tenant before the devices are ordered. Once registered, every device the reseller purchases on your behalf appears in your tenant for auto-enrolment. The device, on first activation, checks in with the platform's enrolment server, identifies its owner organisation, and pulls the MDM enrolment payload before reaching the home screen.

Without this pre-registration, every device requires manual MDM enrolment by your IT team after receipt. Multiplied across a 1,000-device fleet rollout, that's hundreds of hours of IT staging time. Solaris's whole engagement model exists to eliminate that step.

Platforms Solaris coordinates auto-enrolment for

  • Apple Business Manager (DEP): iOS, iPadOS, macOS, tvOS, Apple Watch (via paired iPhone). Solaris registers as the authorised purchaser in your ABM tenant; devices auto-enrol to your MDM (Jamf, Intune, Workspace ONE, Mosyle, Kandji) on first activation.
  • Google Zero-Touch Enrollment: Any Android device from a Zero-Touch participating OEM (Pixel, Samsung, Motorola, Sonim, Kyocera, HMD/Nokia, Transsion, others). Solaris coordinates Zero-Touch registration through registered partner relationships and direct OEM channels.
  • Samsung Knox Mobile Enrollment: Samsung Galaxy and Samsung enterprise devices. Knox-tier configuration depth (Knox security profile, Knox container, Samsung-specific device restrictions) layered alongside Zero-Touch where the deployment is Samsung-heavy.
  • Microsoft Autopilot (for Windows laptops): Pre-registration in your Autopilot tenant for hands-off Windows enterprise enrolment.

MDM platforms Solaris is compatible with

Solaris's auto-enrolment workflow federates with all major MDM platforms: Microsoft Intune, VMware Workspace ONE, Jamf Pro (iOS, macOS, and Android via Jamf Pro adjunct), MobileIron / Ivanti, SOTI MobiControl, Samsung Knox Manage, IBM MaaS360, Mosyle, Kandji, Hexnode, ManageEngine MDM, Scalefusion, and most Apple-supporting and Android Enterprise certified EMM platforms.

How a Solaris auto-enrolment engagement runs

  • Confirmation of your MDM tenant configuration and enrolment profile
  • OEM allocation of the agreed device mix (iPhone, Android by OEM, ruggedised handsets)
  • Pre-shipment registration of every IMEI/serial in your ABM, Zero-Touch and/or Knox tenant
  • Custom OS flashing, SIM lock and carrier branding layered if the deployment requires (MVNO and operator engagements)
  • Asset tagging to your scheme (barcode, QR, RFID per spec)
  • Direct-to-employee fulfilment with chain-of-custody tracking, or depot consolidation if required by compliance
  • Pilot batch validation (typically 5-10 units) before scaling

Combined custom provisioning

For MVNO and operator engagements, Solaris layers Zero-Touch enrolment alongside custom OS flashing, SIM lock to the network, splash-screen branding, default APN configuration, carrier-app pre-installation, and pre-pairing of each handset to the subscriber's SIM and account. Solaris has run this combined provisioning for Republic Wireless, Pacific MVNO operators (including operators serving the Cook Islands), and the Ritual.co restaurant kiosk programme (close to 10,000 mobile-phone kiosk units across three continents).
