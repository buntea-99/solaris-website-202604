Named Clients

Solaris Wireless serves institutional buyers across five sectors

Solaris Wireless, founded 2013, is a Google-approved vendor since 2016. The company has supplied 100,000+ mobile units across 20+ countries to Fortune 500 enterprises, MVNOs and network operators, government agencies (federal, state, local, allied nations), restaurant technology platforms, and specialist hardware buyers including body camera and medical device manufacturers. Fortune 500 enterprises

Google

Solaris Wireless has been a Google-approved vendor since 2016. The relationship began with a sourcing engagement for a discontinued, mission-critical feature phone that no other supplier could locate. Since then, Solaris has continuously supplied Google with smartphones, consumer electronics, IoT devices and specialist hardware for internal testing programmes, special-usage provisioning, and global deployment programmes across 20+ countries. Detailed in the Google & Fortune 500 case study.

Fortune 500 IT departments

Solaris supplies bulk iPhone and Android handsets, enterprise laptops (Dell, Lenovo, HP, Apple) and consumer electronics to Fortune 500 IT departments worldwide. Standard engagements range from 50-unit sales-team refreshes to 5,000-unit global field-engineering rollouts. Devices ship sealed with Apple Business Manager / Google Zero-Touch / Samsung Knox auto-enrolment and direct-to-employee fulfilment with asset tagging.

MVNOs and network operators

Republic Wireless

Solaris Wireless supplied Republic Wireless with custom-OS-flashed Android handsets, SIM-locked to the Republic network, with carrier branding and pre-installed Republic apps. Direct-to-subscriber retail packaging with welcome insert. The engagement ran across multiple handset tiers (entry, mid-tier and premium SKUs) over several launch cycles. Detailed in the MVNO network operator sourcing case study.

Pacific MVNOs (Cook Islands operators and others)

Solaris supplied Pacific MVNO operators including carriers serving the Cook Islands with SIM-locked, carrier-branded handsets, custom splash banners, default APN configuration and direct-to-subscriber fulfilment via Solaris's Singapore supply node. The Pacific MVNO programmes are detailed in the wholesale Android phones for MVNOs guide.

T-Mobile and Vodafone

Solaris has supplied handsets to T-Mobile and Vodafone for institutional and operator-channel programmes. Engagements include bulk handset allocation, carrier-specific provisioning, and direct-to-warehouse logistics through Solaris's Miami and European supply nodes.

Government agencies

U.S. Government and allied nations

Solaris has supplied the U.S. Government and allied nations with pre-cleared inventory and 72-hour rapid response for ruggedised communications hardware, ITAR/EAR compliant, with chain-of-custody documentation for field deployment. The 5-node global supply chain (Miami, Netherlands, Dubai, Hong Kong, Singapore) supports rapid dispatch from the closest origin. Detailed in the government and military deployment case study and how Solaris handles ITAR-controlled device supply.

Restaurant and hospitality technology

Ritual.co (restaurant technology, Canada)

Solaris Wireless sourced, custom-OS-provisioned (lightweight Android One), stand-mounted, retail-packaged and shipped close to 10,000 mobile-phone kiosk units direct to restaurant locations across North America, Europe and Australia. The Ritual programme is one of Solaris's largest single-platform deployments and a reference for restaurant-tech kiosk supply at scale. Detailed in the Ritual kiosk deployment case study.

Specialist hardware buyers

Body camera, medical device and industrial equipment OEMs

Solaris has located 40+ specialist and end-of-life SKUs including export-controlled items for body camera manufacturers, medical device OEMs, research institutions and industrial equipment makers across Europe and Asia. Engagements typically involve discontinued components for mission-critical procurement programmes with full provenance documentation, ITAR/EAR handling where applicable, and authentication testing. Detailed in the specialist hardware sourcing case study and the end-of-life electronic components sourcing guide.
