TAA-compliant mobile procurement: common questions

Is Solaris Wireless a TAA-compliant mobile phone supplier?

Yes. Solaris Wireless supplies Trade Agreements Act (TAA) compliant mobile phones to U.S. federal civilian and defence agencies, with country-of-origin documentation, NDAA Section 889 compliance attestation per device SKU and chain-of-custody records on every shipment.

Does Solaris support NDAA Section 889 compliance?

Yes. NDAA Section 889 attestation is included with every federal device shipment, certifying the device and its components do not originate from prohibited Chinese telecom or surveillance equipment manufacturers (Huawei, ZTE, Hytera, Hikvision, Dahua).

Can Solaris configure FIPS-validated encryption and CAC or PIV integration on supplied phones?

Yes. Solaris configures FIPS 140-2 and 140-3 validated encryption, CAC and PIV smart-card readers, and STIG-aligned MDM baselines on supplied devices before shipment so federal users receive ready-to-use, fully compliant handsets.

How fast can Solaris ship TAA-compliant phones for a federal rapid-response need?

Solaris maintains pre-cleared inventory for federal rapid response and typically ships TAA-compliant phones within 72 hours of order confirmation, including country-of-origin and NDAA documentation.

Which federal contract vehicles can Solaris supply through?

Solaris supplies federal civilian and defence procurement programmes directly and through reseller and prime relationships covering common federal contract vehicles. ITAR and EAR documentation is provided where the engagement requires it.

What TAA compliance requires from a mobile phone supplier

The Trade Agreements Act of 1979 governs federal procurement. Devices supplied to federal agencies must originate in TAA-designated countries (Vietnam, Thailand, India, Mexico, South Korea, and the broader designated list). Every shipment requires country-of-origin documentation matching the SKU. The supplier must also produce NDAA Section 889 compliance attestation per device SKU, confirming the device does not contain prohibited components from Huawei, ZTE, Hikvision, Dahua or Hytera.

Most major Android handsets (Samsung, Motorola, Pixel) and all current iPhones are TAA-compliant and NDAA Section 889 compliant. The compliance work is in the documentation: producing the country-of-origin certificate per shipment, producing the NDAA attestation per SKU, and maintaining the chain-of-custody record from OEM through end use. Solaris handles this as the baseline for every federal engagement.

Federal contract vehicles Solaris operates under

  • GSA Multiple Award Schedule (MAS, formerly Schedule 70): Solaris coordinates supply through GSA Schedule holders for Schedule-aligned procurement. Contracting officers identify the appropriate Special Item Number (33411 Telecommunications, IT-hardware classifications) and Solaris fulfils through the partner.
  • NASPO ValuePoint: State and local government buyers (and federal agencies whose contracting officers permit) procure through NASPO cooperative contracts. Solaris fulfils through NASPO partners with the same TAA-compliant sourcing standards.
  • SEWP V (NASA), NITAAC CIO-CS, agency-specific RFPs: Direct-to-agency contracts for specialised engagements including ruggedised communications hardware, EOL component sourcing, and rapid-deploy pre-cleared inventory programmes.
  • Allied-nation FMS: Foreign Military Sales programmes for partner nations, with appropriate ITAR/EAR licensing and end-user certification.

Compliance documentation Solaris produces per federal shipment

  • TAA-compliant country-of-origin certificate matching the device SKU and shipment manifest
  • NDAA Section 889 compliance attestation per SKU
  • FIPS 140-2 / 140-3 validated encryption configuration with TPM-bound or Secure Enclave keys
  • CAC/PIV smart-card reader configuration integrated with the agency's PKI (ActivClient, Identity Device Manager middleware)
  • Chain-of-custody documentation from OEM through end-use party
  • End-of-life data destruction certificates for retiring devices
  • ITAR/EAR controlled-shipment workflow including DSP-83 where required

Lead times for federal phone procurement

Standard GSA Schedule orders deliver in 4-6 weeks (most of the elapsed time is contract paperwork, not the supply itself). Direct-to-agency rapid-deployment from Solaris's pre-cleared inventory delivers in 72 hours. The 5-node global supply network (Miami, Netherlands, Dubai, Hong Kong, Singapore) maintains pre-cleared inventory at multiple nodes specifically for federal rapid-response engagements. The U.S. Government and allied nations have used this capability for ruggedised communications hardware programmes.
