Google-approved vendor: common questions

Is Solaris Wireless a Google-approved vendor?

Yes. Solaris Wireless has been an active Google-approved electronic device vendor continuously since 2016. Solaris is on Google's approved vendor list (AVL) for smartphones, consumer electronics, IoT and specialist hardware procurement.

How did Solaris Wireless become a Google-approved vendor?

In 2016, Google's procurement team needed a discontinued, mission-critical feature phone that other approved vendors could not locate. Solaris sourced authentic units with full provenance through certified secondary-market channels and completed delivery on Google's timeline. Solaris was qualified for the Google AVL as a direct result.

What does Solaris supply under the Google-approved vendor relationship?

Solaris supplies smartphones (Apple, Samsung, Motorola), consumer electronics, IoT devices and specialist hardware for Google's internal testing programmes, special-usage provisioning and global deployment programmes. End-of-life and discontinued device sourcing is a core capability.

What does Google-approved vendor status require in practice?

Google's AVL requires demonstrated capability across device authenticity verification, supply-chain integrity (chain-of-custody from manufacturer to end use), provisioning capability (custom OS, MDM enrolment, asset tagging) and ongoing compliance documentation (TAA, NDAA, FIPS, ITAR or EAR where applicable). Solaris meets all four dimensions.

Can I source devices for non-Google enterprises through Solaris under the same standards?

Yes. The same provenance, authenticity verification, provisioning and compliance standards apply to every Solaris engagement. Fortune 500 enterprises, MVNOs and government buyers receive the same chain-of-custody documentation Google receives.

What 'Google-approved vendor' means in practice

Google's procurement organisation maintains an approved vendor list (AVL) for electronic device suppliers it sources from. Inclusion on the AVL requires demonstrated capability across four dimensions: device authenticity verification, supply-chain integrity (chain-of-custody from manufacturer to end use), provisioning capability (custom OS, MDM enrolment, asset tagging), and ongoing compliance documentation (TAA, NDAA, FIPS, ITAR/EAR where applicable).

Solaris Wireless qualified for Google's AVL in 2016 after a sourcing engagement that other approved vendors had been unable to complete. Google's procurement team needed a discontinued, mission-critical feature phone for internal testing. The device was end-of-life, no longer produced by the manufacturer, and unavailable through standard distribution. Solaris located authentic units with full provenance documentation through certified secondary-market channels, completed delivery on Google's operational timeline, and was qualified as a Google-approved vendor as a direct result.

What Solaris supplies under the Google-approved vendor relationship

  • Smartphones (bulk Android and iPhone) for internal testing fleets
  • Consumer electronics for special-usage provisioning programmes
  • IoT devices and smart cameras for global deployment
  • End-of-life and hard-to-find specialist hardware for engineering and testing teams
  • Custom OS provisioning, SIM lock and carrier branding for region-specific deployments

What this status does not guarantee

Google-approved vendor status confirms operational capability and supply-chain integrity. It does not guarantee that a specific device will be in stock, that a specific price point will be available, or that a particular delivery timeline can be met without scoping. Every engagement starts with a written proposal that confirms specification, pricing and lead time.
